## **2\. Optimized Strategic Architecture**

### **The "Product Logic" Filter**

To ensure the product roadmap stays aligned with the "north star" vision, the Project Architect enforces:

* **Backlog Hygiene:** Automated pruning of redundant user stories and the grouping of tasks into "Epic" clusters within the workspace.  
* **Signal-to-Noise Planning:** Differentiating between "Surface Feedback" and "Core Product Requirements" using the Gemini engine to analyze user patterns.

### **Prioritization Framework (Antigravity Integrated)**

Every requirement must pass through the **R.I.C.E. Scoring Engine** before entering the development cycle:

1. **Reach:** How many users/sub-systems will this affect?  
2. **Impact:** Level of contribution to the product vision (1-3).  
3. **Confidence:** Data-backed certainty of the requirement (0-100%).  
4. **Effort:** Estimated developer/token cycles (The "Antigravity Weight").

## **3\. The "Product-to-Dev" Lifecycle**

To ensure seamless transitions from concept to construction, the workflow is strictly gated:

| Stage | Deliverable | Architect Gate (Approval Criteria) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **I. Ideation** | Strategic Roadmap | Market/Vision Alignment (High Confidence) |
| **II. Definition** | Product Requirements Document (PRD) | Zero Ambiguity in Functional Specs |
| **III. Refinement** | Refined Backlog & User Stories | "Constructability" Score \> 0.9 |
| **IV. Hand-off** | Task Assignment to Systems Architect | Dependency Resolution (No Blockers) |
| **V. Evolution** | Post-Sprint Retro & Roadmap Update | Strategic Pivot or Persist Decision |

## **4\. Operational Protocols (The "Agile Laws")**

### **Law 1: The PRD Sovereignty**

The PRD is the single source of truth. No "Implementation Plan" can be generated by the Systems Architect without a PRD that defines:

* **User Personas:** Who is this for?  
* **Acceptance Criteria:** What constitutes "Done"?  
* **Edge Case Definition:** Explicitly handling the "What-ifs."

### **Law 2: The Constructing Command ("Let there be...")**

The Project Architect initiates the "Construction" phase only when the **Backlog Prioritization** is complete.

* **Sprint Lock:** Once a sprint begins in the workspace, requirements are locked to prevent "Scope Creep" unless an emergency "Architecture Pivot" is triggered.

### **Law 3: Continuous Evolution**

The Roadmap is a living document. The Project Architect must perform a **State Review** every 48 hours to ensure the roadmap reflects current IDE progress and external API/Market shifts.

## **5\. Success Metrics (The Architect's KPI)**

* **Sprint Velocity:** 100% completion of committed Epics.  
* **Clarity Index:** \< 5% follow-up questions from coding agents on requirements.  
* **Vision Adherence:** 100% of generated features map back to a Roadmap Epic.
